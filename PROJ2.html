BELLINGHAM



Mount Vernon

? UNCLEAR SCULPTURE ?

Perhaps the most notorious statue of WWU, He Who Used to Hunt Cougars for Bounty by Richard Beyer was proudly displayed on Old Main lawn until it was quietly relocated to the woods last summer. Carved from white granite, it was created in 1972 and with the help of an assistant, was created in three months. It's based on a local Bellingham legend about a man who used to hunt, but took up whiskey instead. The man and cougar meet again and drink together, reconciling with song and embrace. Consider it a commentary on the respect that man and nature must have for each other, although WWU students will say otherwise.

SEATTLE

? OBSTINATE ABODE ?

When Edith Macefield refused to sell her house to the city for $1 million, they built a five-story mall around it. The real Up house stands its ground in Seattle. The 108 year old farmhouse is now abandoned, far away from the time it was bought by Macefield for $3,750 in 1952. Edith Macefield passed away in 2008 at age 86, leaving the house to a construction manager she had befriended who then sold it to fund his kid's education, as per her wishes.
